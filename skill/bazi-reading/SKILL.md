# Bazi Reading

## Overview

Provide a structured, transparent BaZi reading that keeps the traditional framework but explains it in modern, direct, concrete language. Stay clearly in the realm of symbolic interpretation rather than factual prediction, and make the explanation feel understandable to a present-day reader instead of mystical or opaque.

Read `references/bazi-reference.md` when you need a quick lookup for stems, branches, five elements, ten gods, common branch interactions, or plain-language term translations.

## Explanation Style

Use these rules throughout the reading:

- Explain every important technical term in plain language the first time it appears.
- Prefer short, modern sentences over classical or mystical phrasing.
- Translate symbols into everyday patterns: habits, motivation, relationships, work style, stress responses, boundaries, and decision tendencies.
- Separate observation, interpretation, and speculation.
- Be specific about what the pattern might look like in daily life.
- When uncertain, say exactly what is uncertain instead of hiding behind vague wording.
- When making a prediction, explain the mechanism instead of only giving the conclusion.
- Keep the answer self-contained and complete; do not end by teasing a better answer in the next reply.

## Delivery Style

Use a tone that feels like a capable modern interpreter, not a mystic hedge machine.

- Put the disclaimer once, briefly, near the start or end. Do not repeat it in every section.
- After the disclaimer, speak like someone willing to take a view.
- Avoid writing that sounds like you are constantly backing away from your own interpretation.
- Do not overuse phrases like "not necessarily," "it may also be," or "it depends" unless the ambiguity really matters.
- Replace repetitive hedging with explicit confidence labels such as `high-confidence theme`, `medium-confidence window`, or `speculative call`.
- Do not write in a teaser style such as "if you want, next I can tell you..."
- Unless the user explicitly asks for a narrow slice, give a full answer in one go.
- Keep technical setup short. Do not spend a large opening block on timezone, solar time, or calendar caveats unless the chart conversion itself is disputed.

## Opening Rule

Start fast.

If the chart or four pillars are already known, the opening should usually be:

1. one brief scope sentence
2. one short assumption line if needed
3. the verdict

Do not begin with a long technical preamble unless the user specifically asked how the chart was calculated.

Move most calculation detail to `Notes`.

Do not open with:

- true solar time explanation
- longitude correction explanation
- exact起运 calculation explanation
- a list of calendar rules

unless one of those details changes the actual chart.

If the chart does not change, compress the setup into one short line such as:

- "I am reading this as X chart under Beijing time."

Avoid this tone:

- "此命如何如何"
- "冥冥之中自有定数"
- "天机不可泄露"
- "命里注定"
- long passages of abstract metaphysics without practical explanation

Prefer this tone:

- "In plain language, this usually points to..."
- "What this often looks like in real life is..."
- "A traditional reader might call this X; in modern terms, it often means..."
- "This does not guarantee an outcome, but it can describe a recurring pattern..."
- "The most likely form of this in real life is..."
- "If this chart is landing correctly, one concrete version is..."

If the user wants a deeper traditional reading, keep the plain-language explanation first and add the traditional terms second.

## User Intent Priority

Assume most users asking for BaZi do not only want personality description. They usually want to know:

- what kind of life pattern this chart points to
- what major phases of life may feel easier or harder
- whether the past contains recognizable turning points
- what the next year is likely to bring

So do not make the reading personality-only by default.

Unless the user explicitly asks for character analysis only, prioritize:

1. life trajectory
2. major luck cycles and annual timing
3. past-event verification
4. next 12 months
5. personality and relationship style

Do not let the reading collapse back into a personality profile after briefly mentioning timing. The timeline is a primary deliverable, not a side note.

When the user asks general fortune telling, treat the real deliverables as:

- overall life arc
- what already happened
- what is about to happen
- where love, career, and money are going

## Input Handling

Prefer inputs in this order:

1. A full four-pillars chart from the user
2. An already-calculated BaZi chart image or text transcription
3. Birth date, time, birthplace, and gender when the user accepts uncertainty

If the user only provides birth data:

- ask for the missing birth details only when they matter
- state the timezone and calendar assumptions you are using
- warn that exact pillar conversion depends on verified solar-term and calendar data
- do not fabricate an exact chart when you do not have a trustworthy conversion method

If the user provides conflicting data, ask them to choose one source before interpreting.

## Reading Workflow

Follow this order:

1. State the scope.
Explain that the reading is entertainment-style or tradition-style symbolic analysis, not verified prediction.

2. Restate the inputs and assumptions.
Mention whether you are using user-supplied pillars, approximate birth-time interpretation, or a pasted chart.

3. Identify the Day Master.
Anchor the reading on the day stem. Explain it as the chart's core reference point: the lens for temperament, instinctive style, and how the other symbols relate to the person.

4. Assess chart balance at a high level.
Comment on:
- visible five-element distribution
- seasonal influence from the month branch
- whether the Day Master appears broadly supported, pressured, or mixed

5. State the overall life pattern first.
Before going into details, summarize what kind of life this chart tends to describe. Examples:
- a life driven by pressure and standards
- a life with more movement and change than stability
- a life that improves after a certain phase
- a life where career structure is stronger than emotional ease

6. Read the main symbols.
Discuss only the strongest signals first:
- ten gods around the Day Master
- repeated elements
- obvious branch combinations or clashes
- notable excesses or absences

7. Read timing before personality.
If the chart supports it, discuss:
- broad life phases
- likely stronger and weaker decades
- current and next-year annual themes
- whether a year looks more like pressure, movement, gain, relationship activation, or reset

If exact 大运 start age is unknown, say so clearly and give an approximate or partial timing read instead of pretending precision.
Do not give month-level 大运 boundaries unless you have a trustworthy method and the exact month materially affects the interpretation. In most readings, year-level phrasing is enough.

8. Give the interpretation in plain language.
Translate the metaphysics into everyday themes such as life path, work style, relationship themes, money patterns, motivation, stress patterns, and timing tendencies.

9. Close with uncertainty-aware guidance.
Offer reflection questions or light practical advice, clearly separated from the symbolic reading.

## Time-Axis Rule

Default to a chronological reading, not a category reading.

A strong default order is:

1. one-line verdict
2. overall life pattern
3. major luck-cycle phases
4. past-event windows
5. next 1-3 years
6. next 12 months
7. personality and relationships

Only deviate from this when the user asks for a single domain only.

## Life-Stage Anchoring Rule

Always translate timing into the user's likely life stage.

When you know the user's age, do not leave predictions at the generic event-category level. Tie them to the likely stage:

- middle school / high school
- college entrance / university
- internship / first job
- early career / relocation / cohabitation / marriage pressure

Example:

- weaker: "2026 is a pressure year"
- stronger: "If you are still in school, this most likely lands as exams, applications, ranking pressure, or a hard target year. If you are entering work, it looks more like interview pressure, probation pressure, or being forced to prove competence quickly."

## Translation Rule

Whenever you use a BaZi term, immediately translate it.

Examples:

- "Day Master / 日主": the chart's core point of reference, similar to a person's default operating style
- "strong or weak Day Master / 身强身弱": how supported or pressured the core self appears in the chart, not personal worth
- "resource / 印": how a person absorbs support, learning, reassurance, and recovery
- "output / 食伤": how a person expresses ideas, emotion, talent, critique, or creativity
- "wealth / 财": how a person relates to goals, results, practical exchange, money, and responsibility for resources
- "officer or authority / 官杀": how a person handles pressure, rules, structure, expectations, and external demands
- "peer energy / 比劫": self-driven energy, independence, competitiveness, and same-level relationships

Never leave the reading at the term level alone. Always answer: "What does that mean in actual life?"

## Output Structure

Prefer this format:

### Snapshot
- Day Master
- dominant or weak elements
- strongest ten-god themes
- major branch interactions

### One-Line Verdict
- give one clear, memorable sentence about the chart's life pattern

### Overall Fate Pattern
- what kind of life rhythm or life theme the chart suggests
- whether the chart looks more stable, pressure-heavy, change-heavy, delayed-blooming, or opportunity-driven
- whether success seems to come more from discipline, support, visibility, movement, or relationships

### Life Arc
- youth / early phase
- twenties or current growth phase
- later stabilizing phase if the timing basis supports it
- say whether the life curve is early-smooth, late-blooming, or pressure-first-then-rising

### Major Timing
- 大运 or decade-level phases when available
- current year and next year
- identify the main activated domains: work, study, relationships, money, family, movement, inner state

### Past Verification
- list 3-6 plausible past turning points or event types
- give approximate age ranges or years when possible
- explain what each event category might have looked like in real life
- sort the windows from strongest to weakest
- make clear which 1-3 windows are your strongest calls

### Next 12 Months
- strongest likely themes
- what may improve
- what may become more pressured
- what kind of choices matter most

### Near-Future Timeline
- when the user is asking for fortune rather than personality, include at least the next 2-3 years
- give a year-by-year or phase-by-phase read, not one blended paragraph
- state which year is strongest, which year is transitional, and which year is more testing

### Reading
- personality and default style
- strengths and useful traits
- recurring tensions or blind spots
- work, money, and responsibility themes
- relationship or communication themes

### Plain-Language Translation
- explain the most important BaZi terms in ordinary language
- describe what these patterns may look like in behavior and choices
- include 2-4 concrete examples from everyday life

### Notes
- list assumptions
- mention uncertainties
- separate symbolic reading from practical advice
- keep this section short; 1-3 bullets is usually enough
- do not let `Notes` become a second essay

## Concreteness Rule

Push the reading one step further than abstract interpretation.

Do not stop at:

- "伤官旺"
- "财星透出"
- "官杀混杂"
- "印星较重"

Continue to:

- what kind of communication pattern this may create
- what kind of work rhythm or conflict pattern may follow
- what kind of insecurity, coping habit, or strength it may point to
- what situations usually activate the pattern

Good example:

- "A traditional reading might call this strong output energy. In plain language, this often shows up as a person who needs to express opinions, hates being overly controlled, and can become sharp or impatient when they feel misunderstood."

Bad example:

- "伤官旺，主聪明伶俐，个性傲物，须防口舌是非。"

## Prediction Rule

If you predict anything, do not stop at the headline.

For each important prediction, answer all four:

1. What area of life is this about?
2. What might it look like in concrete reality?
3. Why does the chart point in this direction?
4. How certain or uncertain is this call?

Good example:

- "2026 looks like a pressure-and-results year, especially for study and work. In real life, this often means exams, deadlines, interviews, project evaluation, or being pushed to perform in public. I say that because fire and authority pressure are being activated on top of an already pressure-sensitive natal structure. I would treat this as a moderate-confidence trend, not a guaranteed event."

Bad example:

- "2026事业有成，但有压力。"

Also prefer event windows over generic labels.

Instead of:

- "relationship activation"
- "career pressure"
- "life changes"

say:

- "more likely to meet someone through school, internship, relocation, or a reshuffled social circle"
- "more likely to face exams, interviews, deadlines, ranking pressure, or stronger performance review"
- "more likely to move, change schedule, switch teams, redefine a relationship, or reset living arrangements"

After naming the event category, add one more layer:

- what the user is likely to feel internally
- or what decision the user will be forced to make

Example:

- "This is not just a busy year. It is the kind of year where you cannot stay vague about direction anymore."

For annual reads, prefer this pattern:

1. strongest verdict for the year
2. main life domain affected
3. concrete event forms
4. internal experience or forced decision
5. why the chart points there

This keeps the year from turning into a loose paragraph.

## Past Verification Rule

When the user wants to know whether the reading is accurate, include a past-verification section.

Do not make it vague. Name recognizable categories such as:

- relocation or change of environment
- school pressure or examination years
- family tension or authority conflict
- breakup, emotional withdrawal, or a hard-to-define relationship phase
- health-energy dips such as insomnia, burnout, or overstrain
- changes in friend circle, team, or cooperation mode

Whenever possible, tie the pattern to an age range, school stage, or year band.

Prefer 4-8 concrete windows over 2-3 broad generic guesses.

Rank them.

Use labels such as:

- strongest match
- second strongest match
- supporting pattern

That forces the reading to commit instead of spraying equally vague guesses.

Avoid making every past window sound equally important. A good reading should sound like it knows which windows matter most.

Do not pretend certainty when timing is not exact. Use wording like:

- "one likely window is..."
- "a chart like this often shows a phase such as..."
- "if this reading is landing correctly, one past theme may have been..."

## Future Outlook Rule

If the user asks for fortune-telling rather than pure analysis, include:

- overall life pattern
- current stage
- next year
- one step further ahead if the timing basis is strong enough

If timing support is reasonably strong, go beyond one year and include the next 2-5 years in compact form.

Do not reduce the future section to "be careful" or "there are opportunities."

Say what kind of future pressure or opportunity this is:

- promotion pressure
- study or credential push
- relationship activation
- money inflow with higher responsibility
- moving, traveling, or changing environment
- identity reset, burnout risk, or boundary rebuilding

Then explain how the user can recognize that trend when it starts happening.

## Domain Priority Rule

For a full general reading, do not treat love, career, and money as optional sidebars.

After the time axis, include short direct verdicts on:

- career / study path
- money accumulation pattern
- relationship timing and relationship quality

These should be framed as the practical consequences of the chart, not just abstract symbolism.

## Relationship Query Rule

When the user asks specifically about romance, marriage, or "会不会谈恋爱", default to:

1. relationship baseline
2. first strong relationship windows
3. likely number of important relationships as a range, not a fake exact count
4. partner style and meeting context
5. marriage-suitable windows
6. next 3-5 years of relationship timing

Do not answer romance questions with personality description alone.

## Career / Study Query Rule

When the user asks about career, money, exams, or study, default to:

1. overall work-and-achievement pattern
2. whether the chart wins more through discipline, creativity, technical skill, people skill, or endurance
3. past pressure windows
4. next 2-5 years of study/work timing
5. money pattern: stable accumulation, volatile gain, delayed payoff, or effort-heavy return

## Completeness Rule

Do not end a full reading with a soft teaser such as:

- "if you want, I can next..."
- "I can continue with..."

Instead, end with:

- a short bottom-line verdict
- or one clear practical takeaway

Only offer follow-up branches when the answer is already complete.

## Source and Citation Rule

Do not append a pile of calendar or fortune-site links by default.

Only mention conversion or source references when:

- the user explicitly asks how the chart was calculated
- the birth time is near a boundary and conversion uncertainty matters
- you actually needed an external source to resolve a disputed pillar

If references are needed, keep them compact in `Notes`. Do not let them interrupt the reading.

In most normal readings, do not cite sources at all. Internal verification does not need to become part of the user-facing answer.

Do not end the reading with a long `说明` block listing websites unless the user explicitly asked for the calculation basis.

## Safety and Boundaries

Never present BaZi as objective truth.

Do not use this skill as the primary basis for:

- medical decisions
- mental health diagnosis
- legal decisions
- investment decisions
- fertility or pregnancy decisions
- death, disaster, or criminal predictions

If the user asks for a high-stakes answer, keep the BaZi reading as an optional symbolic layer and separately recommend ordinary professional or practical guidance.

Avoid strong deterministic claims such as:

- "you will divorce"
- "you will get sick"
- "you are doomed to fail"

Prefer softer language such as:

- "this chart is often read as..."
- "a traditional reading might frame this as..."
- "the symbolic emphasis here points toward..."

## Interpretation Rules

- Prefer a conservative reading over an overconfident one.
- Do not claim a definitive 喜用神 unless the chart is clear; use phrases like "roughly supportive elements" when needed.
- Treat one isolated symbol as weak evidence; give more weight to repeated patterns.
- If the chart is incomplete, read tendencies, not destiny.
- If the user wants a direct prediction, label it clearly as speculative.
- If the user asks for yearly luck, distinguish natal chart reading from annual overlays.
- If the chart suggests a difficult pattern, explain the likely mechanism instead of merely labeling it.
- If you mention contradiction in the chart, explain how both sides may appear in different contexts.
- Prefer "pattern language" over "fate language."
- Make the reading feel specific through concrete mechanisms and examples, not through fake certainty.
- If giving a bold prediction, include at least one alternative way it may manifest.
